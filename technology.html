Robotics Surgery in the age of pandemics

At the age of COVID-19 where it has caused us to distant from each other, it is a threat to surgeons. Surgical surgery is one of the main foundation of any healthcare system with both elective and emergency operations contributing to the health services of the population. Operatin rooms are potentially high-risks areas for transmission due to the involvement of multiple teams, and high-risk activties.

To effectively reduce the spread, AI robotic surgery can be integrated at several sections of the sequence, which each surgical patient traverses during a hospital stay. Allowing for the workflow to be divided into pre-operative care, anaesthesia, the surgical procedure and postoperative care, the overall goal of this is to minimize contact between patient and healthcare provider at each step.

AI Robotic surgery has its benefits and risks, depending on the stakeholders. For example, nn terms of New Zealand patients/public it will mean easier and faster Healthcare services for people to access and can be in places healthcare workfadce can't such as rural setting. However, access to this technology may not yet be publicly funded, out of private finanical reach for some individuals. Additionally, robots aren't always the answer, it has to be useful to the patient.
